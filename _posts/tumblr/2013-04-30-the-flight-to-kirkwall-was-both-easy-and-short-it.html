The flight to Kirkwall was both easy and short. It essentially was just boarding, having a cup of tea with a biscuit, and then preparing for landing. Getting my luggage and hopping on the local bus into town was just as quick and painless, and before I knew it the driver was very kindly directing me to where my lodgings were located.

After situating myself in my barren but very comfortably sized room at the Peedie Hostel, I decided to take a walk around to find something to eat.

My feet, however, had other plans than my stomach. After taking a wrong turn, I ended up taking probably an hour’s walk though the more residential bit of Kirkwall.

All of those houses look primarily the same. They are built out of some sort of cement like mix that seems to be mostly small pebbles.

The quaintest bits are the small window dressings and doorways that make each house a bit more distinct.

There seem to be a fair amount of churches here… I counted three on my short walk. The most impressive those was the St. Magnus cathedral, a large red brick building that looks both well worn and well taken care of.

Next to the St. Magnus cathedral, I found two castle-like structures which, upon further inspection, the Bishop and Earl’s palaces from who knows how long ago.

Upon further wandering, I finally managed to figure out where I was on the tiny map provided to me by the free guidebook I picked up from the hostel.

Noticing that it was near closing time for most of the restaurants, I high tailed it to a local fish and chips place called the Harbor Fry. £5,50 got me two large pieces of fried fish and a healthy portion of French fries. Believe me, I was quite enthused; the packaging alone was something I found to be quite delightful. The fish, though greasy, was very lightly fried compared to the stuff I am used to from back home, and the addition of salt and vinegar by the fry cook was incredibly tasty.

Coming back to my room with my cardboard boxclroudly proclaiming that I was to be eating Fish and chips, real food. 150 years of fish ‘n’ chips and still the nation’s favorite, I have been watching a soccer game and Jools Holland. If that doesn’t prove I’m in the UK I don’t know what would…

I don’t think it had quite set in yet that I am actually in a different country doing things that I find to be incredibly interesting and exciting. The only thing I’ve really felt so far is a dash of embarrassment because of my accent, and excitement for plans that I have for the coming weeks of the trip.

Maybe that just means that I am living in the moment.
